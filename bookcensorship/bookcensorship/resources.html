Free Book Resources

As technology has become fully integrated in our everyday lives, it’s gotten more and more commonplace to find an activity that has a technological alternative. One prominent example is reading. According to a survey conducted by the Pew Research Center, “One-fifth of American adults (21%) report that they have read an e-book in the past year.” An e-book is a book provided in a digital format. E-books can be accessed via the internet, applications, or on a plethora of devices with the option to read electronically, or e-read. E-reading refers to reading on portable devices such as smartphones, tablets, laptops, and devices geared around reading electronically, or e-readers, such as Kindles and Nooks. With the emergence of e-reading, reading has become more accessible as with e-reading technology you can adjust the settings your book will display in, share books with more ease, use interactive elements, and not worry about the general upkeep of your book(s).

As book bans surge, it is important to have access to free e-reading resources. Below are some of my favorite sites and/or applications for reading for free.

Libby

"Libby is a free app where you can enjoy ebooks, digital audiobooks, and magazines from your public library. You can stream titles with Wi-Fi or mobile data, or download them for offline use and read anytime, anywhere. All you need to get started is a library card."

Hoopla

"Borrow and enjoy audiobooks, eBooks, comics, movies, TV, magazines, or music everywhere you have a screen-your computer, your phone, your car, even your TV. All you need is a library card. hoopla syncs across all your devices, so you can stream titles immediately or whenever you're in the mood. Most titles can also be downloaded to your phone or tablet. hoopla offers more content, in more places, than any other digital library platform and it's all FREE thanks to your public library! Titles may vary based on library catalog."

Project Gutenberg

"Choose among free epub and Kindle eBooks, download them or read them online. You will find the world’s great literature here, with focus on older works for which U.S. copyright has expired. Thousands of volunteers digitized and diligently proofread the eBooks, for you to enjoy."
