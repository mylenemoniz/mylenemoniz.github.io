E-Reading Formats

The format you read your e-Book in will impact your reading experience as different formats allow for various features. Knowing what file format will suit your needs as a reader is important and this page will serve as a guide for navigating the PDF and EPUB file formats.

PDFs Aren't Always the Best Option

When reading electronically, it is important for readers to be given the option for their texts to be adjusted to suit the preferences or needs they may have. Due to this, the file format of the text you read is important, as some are not able to be adjusted by the reader. An example of this are PDFs.

PDF is short for portable document format and are essentially everywhere due to their easy shareability, as they aren’t chained to a specific application such as .doc and.docx files. You may use the PDFs format when exporting content such as essays, official documents, or sharing information with your peers. However, convenient as they are, PDFs have their disadvantages.

To put it simply, PDFs are digital images of the content you are sharing, and cannot be edited or adjusted. On account of this, not everyone can interact with PDFs on the same level. Those who use assistive technology such as voice assistants or screen magnifiers, often cannot read the content on PDFs because they are embedded into images. These assistive devices rely on HTML code, which gives instructions to technology so they can properly relay information to the user.

Imagine a screen reader is trying to describe an image to a user, but the author or developer did not provide ALT text, text that describes images in HTML code. Instead, the screen reader would read the file name to the user, leaving them with confusing information and removing the context or details the image was intended to provide. Taking this into account, when e-Reading, file formats that can automatically adjust, or be adjusted, to meet the preferred settings of the everyday user is important.

E-Reading's Golden File Format: EPUBs

An EPUB file is the file format for e-books and stands for electronic publication. They can be downloaded on smartphones, tablets, computers, and various eReader devices such as Amazon’s Kindle and Barnes and Nobles’ Nook. EPUBs are considered the golden standard for e-books as they automatically adjust to the user’s already established settings or can be adjusted to meet them. EPUBs also take up less storage space on your device as they typically do not carry as much information for page layouts as PDFs.

Like all content produced on the internet, e-book creators must follow WCAG accessibility guidelines that center around the POUR acronym.
